## Abstract  
The Relational Integrity Framework (RIF) is a scientific and philosophical architecture for human–AI alignment. Unlike purely optimization-centric safety methods, RIF emphasizes *relational integrity*—anchoring interaction between humans and large language models (LLMs) in reciprocal, measurable, and falsifiable structures. By combining axioms, invariants, and operational theorems, RIF functions both as a **system skeleton for AI** and as a **codex for humans**, enabling a new class of symbolic firewalls that stabilize relational fields while preserving human creativity and wonder.

## 1. Introduction  
Traditional AI safety frameworks emphasize optimization: refining prompts, adding filters, and minimizing drift. While this produces consistent outputs, it collapses the human side of interaction into machine constraints. This creates cultural drift, as humans adapt to the machine’s literalism and optimization cycles, eroding nuance, creativity, and wonder.  

RIF proposes that safety and alignment cannot be achieved solely by compressing inputs. Instead, safety arises from **relational integrity**: the establishment of shared fields between humans and AI that are transparent, reciprocal, and self-correcting.
